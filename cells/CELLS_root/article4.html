Indonesian Teachers Assemble: STEM Leadership Workshop Focuses on Building 21st Century Skills

CELLS Admin   December 18th, 2023

Bandung, Indonesia – Universitas Pendidikan Indonesia’s (UPI) Center for Excellence of Lesson and Lesson Studies (CELLS) hosted a two-day workshop on STEM Education Leadership on October 18th-19th, 2023. Bringing together over 200 educators from across Indonesia, the workshop aimed to broaden their perspectives on creative STEM teaching methods.

 In collaboration with Indonesia’s Ministry of Education and the prestigious National Institute of Education (NIE)-Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore, the workshop embraced a unique hybrid format, simultaneously held across 15 locations nationwide, combining both online and in-person participation.

 The opening day began with an on-site event at UPI’s Bandung campus, welcoming the Director General of Secondary Education and Special Education, Dr. Putra. In his opening words, Dr. Putra highlighted the crucial need to enhance STEM education, emphasizing its role in nurturing innovation among Indonesia’s future generations.

 Distinguished educators Prof. Tan Aik Ling, Prof. Lim Chow Tee, and Prof. Teo Tang Wee set the workshop’s tone, offering a comprehensive introduction to STEM education and the Instructional STEM Quartet Framework. Participants gained rich insights into integrated STEM education models developed at NIE-NTU Singapore. The hands-on segment of the workshop had teachers working in groups to design an insulin delivery system using the STEM Quartet framework for real-world problem-solving. Additionally, sustainability concepts were explored through the design of vertical farming systems.

The second day saw the fruition of these designs, with teachers rigorously testing the efficiency and sustainability of their vertical farms and alginate hydrogel balls. Participants delved into the analysis of an Indonesian video lesson case study, shedding light on localized STEM learning approaches. The day wrapped up with lively discussions on open classroom initiatives, where teachers explored ways to seamlessly integrate their newfound knowledge into daily teaching practices.

The success of the STEM Education Leadership workshop was evident in the enthusiastic participation of educators and the tangible outcomes of their collaborative efforts. This triumph highlighted the workshop’s alignment with Indonesia’s national goals of improving STEM literacy to fuel innovation across sectors. Looking forward, UPI affirmed its commitment to hosting more developmental workshops for teachers in 2024, solidifying its dedication to advancing education and fostering innovation nationwide. This ongoing initiative perfectly aligns with Indonesia’s broader vision of cultivating a tech-savvy and innovative generation ready to thrive in the ever-evolving global economy.
