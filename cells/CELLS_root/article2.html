Global Insights Unleashed: Streaming Lesson Study Workshop with Japan’s IDCJ

CELLS Admin   December 4th, 2023

Bandung, September 5th-6th, 2023 – Esteemed Japanese experts, Professor Atsushi Tsukui and Mr. Kirie Miyajima, were warmly welcomed by the Center for Excellence in Lesson and Learning Studies (CELLS) in early September. They showcased cutting-edge equipment and techniques designed to enhance the streaming of lesson study sessions in Bandung.

 The innovative workshop aimed to transform lesson study by utilizing platforms such as ZOOM to make open classes accessible online. This opens up opportunities for distant teachers and enthusiastic education enthusiasts to actively participate and engage.

 On September 5th, the Center for Excellence in Lesson and Learning Studies (CELLS) UPI hosted a highly anticipated workshop on streaming lesson study sessions. The event drew 18 participants, including lecturers and students, and took place at the DIPUU Building. Renowned Japanese expert Professor Atsushi Tsukui headlined the workshop, further enriching the learning experience.

 Professor Tsukui showcased state-of-the-art Mini Pro and Switch devices, demonstrating their seamless capabilities for broadcasting open classes online. He provided insights into functionalities and highlighted key requirements, such as internet bandwidth. The IDCJ team complemented this by presenting ideal setups that included cameras, microphones, and tripods. They detailed standard specifications and optimal configurations for educational technology.

 The commitment to propel lesson study through live-streaming platforms aligns with CELLS’ vision of enhancing accessibility and visibility for teaching practitioners and education enthusiasts in Indonesia.

 The workshop began with the IDJC team delivering a comprehensive introductory lesson on the optimized implementation of lesson study. They detailed protocols for pre-, during, and post-open class activities, outlining the intricate roles involved at each stage. The session provided significant guidance on refining observation techniques and configuring equipment to ensure optimal recording for research purposes. This underscores the commitment to fostering a rich, research-driven educational environment.

 The detailed overview of orchestrating meaningful lesson study highlights its growing influence in Bandung and beyond. Stakeholders emphasized the importance of streamlining protocols to systematically gather audiovisual materials during open classes, further supporting classroom-based research.

 On September 6th, IDJC and CELLS-UPI organized an open class at Nur Al Rahman Junior High School in Cimahi City to debut the streaming lesson study capabilities showcased in previous workshops. The seamless collaboration between Japanese experts and local stakeholders laid the groundwork for the open class’s smooth execution. The organic maiden implementation of streaming lesson study at Nur Al Rahman Junior High School holds the promise of more widespread adoption, enhancing professional teacher development.

 On September 6th, the collaborative teams from IDJC and CELLS-UPI arrived at the school for thorough pre-class preparations. This included the meticulous setup of streaming equipment and final checks in the classroom. The open class unfolded in an engaging 7th-grade science session on “Physical and Chemical Properties of Substances,” led by the model teacher, Ms. Nadia, in front of 20 vibrant students.

 Ms. Nadia captivated attendees by incorporating active demonstrations and PowerPoint visuals. A 10-member observation team, comprising school teachers and CELLS members, took positions armed with lesson plans and observation sheets. Moderator-led pre-class briefings outlined focuses such as learning objectives to align notes.

 Following the Merdeka curriculum, the science class conducted in the Indonesian language unfolded over an engaging 90-minute duration, meticulously outlined in both the lesson plan and recording schedule. This comprehensive timeframe covered activities from the introduction to the closure, seamlessly aligning with the planned lesson progression.

 The session, rich in educational content, witnessed the model teacher skillfully guiding students through a review of prior concepts, elucidating learning objectives, and emphasizing the practical benefits of the lesson. An integral part of the experience involved students actively observing phenomena in their daily lives before delving into the formal concept presentation.

 The central focus of the class revolved around hands-on experiments related to Physical and Chemical Properties, with students organized into groups. Notably, for the purpose of the lesson study, each student and group received a distinct label and corresponding recorded identification numbers, contributing to the meticulous documentation of this innovative educational endeavor.

 After the culmination of the lesson, a reflective session took place in the classroom during recess, bringing together all observers and the esteemed model teacher. The moderator skillfully led the session, providing the model teacher with a platform to express her thoughts and sentiments regarding the immersive experience of the open class and the lesson study process.

 This encompassed a comprehensive assessment of whether the anticipated learning objectives were met, along with an insightful exploration of the challenges encountered throughout the pedagogical journey.

 After an enriching reflection session, the IDJC team dedicated valuable time to introduce the innovative concept of lesson study streaming to the school’s committed teachers. A demonstration unfolded, showcasing optimal equipment alongside suggestions for more budget-friendly alternatives. The lesson study session concluded on a harmonious note, capturing a collective group photo that fostered a sense of camaraderie and provided a platform for networking between the teachers and the collaborative teams.

 This collaborative exchange holds the potential for continual enhancements in teaching practices and an elevated standard of education within the school.
