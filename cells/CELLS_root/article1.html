SOUTHEAST ASIA (SEA) WORKSHOP: Professional Learning Community Through Lesson Study

CELLS Admin   November 30th, 2023

Bandung, June 11-18, 2023 – The Center for Excellence of Lesson and Learning Studies at the Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences Education, Universitas Pendidikan Indonesia (FPMIPA UPI), in collaboration with the Ministry of Education of the Kingdom of Malaysia (KPM) and Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM), successfully organized the Southeast Asia (SEA) Workshop on Professional Learning Community Through Lesson Study. The week-long workshop, held from June 11 to June 18, 2023, located in Bandung, Indonesia, brought together 29 participants consisting of Malaysian teachers, lecturers, and ministry officials for an enriching exchange of educational expertise.

Enculturation to Paris van Java

 On Sunday, June 11, participants safely arrived in Indonesia. The day was dedicated to fostering a sense of cultural understanding and exploration as participants visited Cihampelas Street, immersing themselves in the local culture, including exploring unique crafts and foods. This experience set the tone for the days ahead, fostering a connection between participants and the cultural tapestry of Indonesia that would weave its way through their time in Bandung.

Orientation and Knowledge Exchange

 The event commenced with welcoming remarks from representatives of UKM, KPM, and UPI, followed by the signing of Memorandum of Agreement (MoA) and Implementing of Agreement (IA). The academic sessions began with presentations on “Teaching Gap and Learning Poverty: A Pinpoint of Lesson Study” by Dr. Arif Hidayat and Dr. Adi Rahmat. How the world data and trends lead to pinpoint of Lesson Study in the World Book and Journal discussion was explored by reviewing current research and trends in the field of education. This provided an understanding of the most pressing issues within education and how Lesson Study can help address them.

 Subsequent discussion covered “Defining Lesson Study as Teacher Professional Development” and delivered by Prof. Sumar Hendayana & Prof. Riandi. Initial activities of lesson study originated back in Japan in the early 1900s, but have since then become a form of Teacher Professional Development. Lesson Study has been adapted to suit the needs of different contexts, such as in Indonesia, where it has been successful in improving the quality of education. It has also proven to be an effective way for teachers to collaborate and learn from each other.

 The last lecture for the day discussed the “Modern Pedagogy through Lesson Study’’ as delivered by Dr. Asep Supriatna and Dr. Eni Nuraeni. This lecture highlighted the importance of Lesson Study in fostering creativity and collaboration among teachers. It also provided insight into how Lesson Study can be used to create an effective learning environment for students. Using the video case in Indonesia and its reflection, the discussion led to the need of lesson study. The lecture provided teachers with the opportunity to discuss the challenges and successes of implementing the Lesson Study in their respective schools and to learn from each other’s experiences.

Experiencing Lesson Study by Visiting Indonesian Public School & City Government

 Participants delved into an engaging school visit at SMPN 1 Cimahi, where the focus extended beyond mere observation to active participation in the reflective exploration of students’ learning experiences. This carefully orchestrated visit unfolded with participants immersing themselves in classrooms, gaining firsthand insights into diverse teaching methodologies and the dynamic interplay between educators and students. The pivotal session centered on a dual approach—observing and actively participating in reflections—offering a comprehensive understanding of the intricacies of students’ learning journeys.

 The schedule was continued as the Mayor’s Representative from Cimahi District Mayor Office, Dr. Syawal, delivered a compelling address on the latest educational policies in Indonesia. Following this, Encik Akhirudin took the stage to present a thorough Educational Policy Update for Malaysia, emphasizing the collaborative spirit of the event and promoting cross-border knowledge exchange.

 The day further went with an insightful agenda focused on District Commitment and Learning Community Initiatives. The Head of the District Education Office took center stage, articulating the intricacies of how principals, school supervisors, and the district education office collaboratively initiated learning community initiatives within individual schools and across the district. Engaging in dialogues with these key stakeholders, the session not only underscored the commitment to fostering a collaborative learning environment but also provided a platform for shared experiences, challenges, and innovative solutions.

Lessons and Development Insights

 The professional development itinerary unfolded with a series of engaging sessions led by distinguished educators. The first segment, led by Prof. Tatang Herman, Prof. Dr. Nahadi, and Dr. Dadi Rusdiana, delved into the initiation and sustained commitment to lesson study in Sumedang Districts. Their insights included a compelling case-sharing from public schools, illustrating the collaborative efforts involving teachers, principals, school supervisors, board of education, house representatives, and parents’ representatives. This session extended to prospective discussions on implementing lesson study in the Malaysian context, fostering valuable dialogue among participants. Following this, participants actively engaged in group discussions aimed at formulating step-by-step strategies for introducing lesson study tailored to the unique educational contexts of both Indonesia and Malaysia.

 In the subsequent segment, Prof. Sumar Hendayana explored the nuances of high-quality lesson study by analyzing a video case from Malaysian lessons. Participants were encouraged to reflect on the spot, identifying elements contributing to the quality of lesson delivery and subsequent reflections. Dr. Arif Hidayat then led the discussion on lesson design, offering participants the opportunity to redesign reflected lessons based on hypothetical learning trajectories, thereby enhancing their understanding of effective pedagogical approaches.

 Concluding the day, Dr. Asep Supriatna guided participants in preparing actionable plans, emphasizing the pivotal role of principals and the technical aspects involved upon their return to Malaysia. This comprehensive program not only provided valuable insights but also equipped participants with practical tools to elevate their educational practices.

School Visit: A Reflective Exploration

 The day commenced with a visit to Nur Al Rahman Primary School, providing participants with a hands-on opportunity to observe and actively engage in reflections on student learning. Following a shared session, a dedicated segment exclusively for participants allowed for a more personalized exploration of the observed lessons and their pedagogical implications.

 Dr. Arif Hidayat led a reflective discussion on “Slow Knowledge and Slow Pedagogy,” focusing on specific content derived from findings in the open lesson and teacher discussions. The session underscored the vital connection between lessons, students’ characters, and educational programs. The day concluded with Dr. Utari Wijayanti and Dr. Arif Hidayat facilitating a discussion on shaping perspectives through lesson analyses. Utilizing a case lesson from Cimahi, the session enhanced participants’ analytical skills, connecting these insights with previous reflections at Cimahi Secondary School. This activity aimed to deepen analytical capabilities and strengthen the alignment between pedagogical theories and practical teaching approaches.

Lesson Study Integration in Teacher Education

 The workshop unfolded with a session led by Prof. Aan Komariah and Dr. Harun Imansyah, where they, as Teacher Profession Education Experts and Practitioners, detailed the seamless integration of Lesson Study into Teacher Education Profession (PPG). This informative segment elucidated the incorporation of Lesson Study in elected courses and teaching practices, highlighting its significance in shaping effective teaching methodologies.

 Following this, Prof. Sumar Hendayana and Dr. Harun Imansyah facilitated a presentation of Action Plans crafted by the participants. This interactive session involved insightful commentary from peers and UPI Scholars, fostering a collaborative environment for constructive feedback and refinement of each action plan. Dr. Arif Hidayat then wrapped up the workshop’s contents, emphasizing the linkage between various segments, showcasing the progress made by participants, and providing a general review of the action plans.

 The workshop reached its end with a Closing and Participant Reflection session, featuring a farewell greeting from the participants and a closing speech by UPI’s Rector, Prof. Solehuddin. This farewell also included the awarding of certificates, creating a memorable concluding moment captured with a group photograph. The day’s events not only enriched participants with valuable insights but also fostered a sense of collaborative growth and accomplishment.

Cultural Exploration and Farewell

 On the last day, participants experienced the rich cultural milieu of Bandung, embarking on visits to Saung Angklung Udjo and Pasar Baru. Saung Angklung Udjo provided an illuminating exploration into the meticulous craft of angklung, complemented by a captivating angklung performance that left participants mesmerized. The day culminated with an excursion at Pasar Baru, affording participants the chance to window shopping and purchase souvenirs for their loved ones. As the participants left Bandung, they concluded their visit to UPI Bandung, carrying not just a handful of good knowledge obtained during the SEA Workshop but also remember the cultural experiences that would be cherised well beyond their departure.
