Now Everyone Can Code: STEM Workshop Shows Teachers the Possibilities

CELLS Admin   December 30th, 2023

On January 25th, 2024, the enthusiasm from December’s Jakarta workshop carried over to a follow-up “Hands-On STEM Coding” event held at Nur Al Rahman School in Cimahi. Eager primary and secondary teachers from the greater Bandung and Cimahi regions attended, ready to build on their coding skills.

 Dr. Arif Hidayat, Head of the Center for Excellent Lesson and Learning Studies (CELLS) and President of the Indonesia Lesson Study Association (ALSI), expanded on coding’s integral role in innovative lesson planning and student development in his lecture on the landscape of STEM education globally and in Indonesia.

 WoW Learning! Singapore then introduced highly practical methods for integrated STEM coding classrooms. Educators worked through coding fundamentals using provided worksheets, gaining firsthand experience building traffic light systems with Artec Co., Ltd. Japan’s STEAM kits—to positive feedback.

 The focus then shifted to secondary coding techniques using Python where teachers collaboratively constructed a self-driving car prototype. Artec’s STEAM kits feature modules with varying complexity to align with students’ progress.

 Empowered by the hands-on learning, enthusiastic teachers incorporated workshop learnings into curriculums and signed up for future training. The workshop’s success highlighted the strengthening collaboration between schools, government, and innovative partners like Artec Japan and WoW Learning Singapore to make STEM education in Indonesia more engaging, creative, and impactful.
